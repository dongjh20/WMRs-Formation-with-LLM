Demonstration for WMRs-Formation

WMRs-Formation here refers to the formation control of wheeled mobile robots (WMRs) integrating Large Language Models (LLMs).

Real-world experiments

1. Formation navigation

Seven robots arranged in three columns move in a hexagonal formation.

2. Autonomous obstacle avoidance during formation navigation

The two outermost robots balance formation maintenance and obstacle avoidance simultaneously. This capability enhances the adaptability and flexibility of the WMRs formation in dynamic environments, as the surroundings are not always open and free of obstacles.

3. Formation switching

The formation of seven WMRs switches from three columns to two. Such switching enhances the WMRs formation' adaptability to dynamic environments. For example, sudden changes in navigable areas represent one type of dynamic environments where the formation must adjust accordingly.

Experiments in simulated environments

During formation switching, the WMRs formation encounter obstacles and is able to avoid them while switching to the new formation. Our method demonstrates strong adaptability to dynamic environments.

Abstract

Formation control of wheeled mobile robots (WMRs) has been extensively studied for its wide applications in many fields, such as logistics transportation, resource exploration, and etc. However, previous work are mainly focused on relatively simple scenarios with fixed formation configurations, which significantly limits their flexibility and adaptability in complex, dynamic environments. This paper aims to develop a flexible and adaptive formation control approach for WMRs in dynamic environments with formation switching. To accomplish this, we propose a semi-distributed architecture that combines centralized assignment to resolve conflicts with distributed individual robot planning to ensure robustness and efficiency. Specifically, we utilize Large Language Models (LLMs) with expert action suggestions to understand the dynamic environments and adaptively generate new formations. On the other hand, we develop a spatiotemporal joint trajectory optimization method for the WMRs, utilizing their differential flatness properties and the latest trajectory representation method, which simultaneously coordinates the spatiotemporal relationships of multiple WMRs. Simulations and real-world robot experiments validate the effectiveness of our approach, highlighting its flexibility and adaptability in WMRs formation tasks. The experimental videos are available at https://dongjh20.github.io/WMRs-Formation-with-LLM.
